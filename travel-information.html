Fernando & Hannah

Wifi

Free wifi is available in most public places in Sydney and in Melbourne. You may be asked to supply your full name and email address upon connection.

Accommodation

AirBNB - Renting a room or apartment

This is the best value for money option, go on this website to rent a room or an apartment. This option seems to be the cheapest as they are rentals directly from the landlord, waiving the real estate agent fee. Ensure the quality by reading reviews.

Visit Website

Wotif

A website that provides reservation service for hotels. This is easy to navigate to help you find the hotel best suited for you and your needs. If you are up for a surprise try a "Mystery Hotel" in the Melbourne CBD as they often turn out to be fun and good value for money!

Visit Website

Serviced Apartments

The following serviced apartments/hotels are a great option for those who want to save money by staying with their family / wedding attendee.

 

Oaks on Lonsdale - offers 3 bedroom apartments with 3 kings or 1 double and twin beds (6 guests) ~524$ per night

 

Oaks on Collins - offers 2 bedroom apartments with two queen beds (4 guests) ~349$ per night

 

Milano - offers 2 bedroom apartments that can hold up to 5 guests ~259$ per night

 

Parkville Place Apartments - offers 2 bedrooms (6 guests) ~220$ per night

Travel and Transportation

Going around Melbourne is relatively easy as it is a very walkable city.

 

If you find walking is not for you, free travel on trams in the Melbourne CBD make getting around and exploring easy too! Beyond the free tram zone, you will have to buy a Myki card. This is the travel ticket for any Melbourne public transport - buses, trams (outside of free tram zone), and trains.

 

Visit Website

Uber is a very reliable taxi service in most parts of the world, Melbourne included. Get this App on your smart phone to hail an UBER taxi with one convenient tap. It can give you a quote for the ride if you enter in your destination.

Things to do

A trip to the snow will be good activity at this time of the year, especially if you want to ski or just to experience the snow. If you’re a wine afficionado, Yarra Valley is the place to go. However, if you’re just interested in exploring the city, here are some recommendations based on the couple’s favourites:

 

 

Breakfast/Brunch/Coffee!!!

 

Melbourne has arguably one of the best coffees in the world and you can try all the quaint little cafes around. If you are not having coffee on the go, try to enjoy a weekend brunch over these favorites of ours!

 

Campos - has surely captured us with their awesome flat whites and their varied brunch menu! Tip: If you see sourdough, guava, and scrambled eggs in the menu, just get it!

 

Seven seeds -can be very packed so be patient, especially if you want to try or favourite—the avocado smash!

 

Auction Rooms - Please ask for their coffee of the day and you will never fail. Brûlée french toast is a must partner for your coffee!

 

Manchester press - Must try: salads (apple and pancetta) and open bagels (smoked salmon)!

 

Hardware Societe - come around 11am on a lazy Sunday morning and you’re in for a French fare.

 

Common ground/Dolcetti - usually our go-to cafes after the mass at our wedding venue. Dolcetti also has some hearty soups which is perfect for winter. Look out for free apples by the door of Common Ground!

 

 

 

Late Lunch/Dinner

 

We don't usually eat out but we have found very good places and the best part is they are student friendly!

Here are some of our favorites:

 

Shanghai Street - the best Xiao Long Bao everrrr!

 

Thai Culinary - everything to be honest! Good quality food with good serving for a good price too! And who could wrong with a Thai milk tea, seriously?

 

Mekong - place can be a bit crowded and busy but the Chicken Pho will definitely make you feel all warm and fuzzy!

 

Papa Rich - must-try: aside from the Milo dinosaur, Roti and the Hainanese chicken are our faves.

 

Papa Gino’s - must-try: Gino's special and the mushroom pizza if you love fungi. If you’re a little adventurous, you can try the Australian Pizza  and find out what makes it different!

 

D.O.C - must-try: everything! But if you must really choose, the truffle mushroom pizza! The buffalo mozzarella are yummy appetizers too!

 

Universal - must-try: chicken parma with chips and salad--very affordable meal for a huge appetite!

 

 

Dessert

 

We love home-baked desserts but we also love going out for desserts for celebrations. Here are the ones on the top of our list: (tip: must try-everything! Just go there and you'll know what we mean!)

 

Our favorite is Hopetoun Tea Rooms. But we also recommend: Brunetti’s, Koko Black, Lindt Cafe, Max Brenner, and San Churros.

 

Other activities:

Sightseeing

 

Most of Melbourne’s sights can be accessed via the free hop on-hop off City Circle Tram. Fernando and Hannah’s personal favourites are the Royal Botanic Gardens, Yarra River, and the Eureka Skydeck.

 

Shopping

 

June is the end of financial year in Australia so if you are arriving in June, you’re in for a great sale destination. There might be a few leftovers come early July so it wouldn’t hurt to be on the lookout. If you are planning to spend over $300 in one shop and want to get approximately 10% tax refund, be sure to keep the receipt. If you are spending over $1000 in one shop, make sure to ask the retailer to put your name and address on the tax invoice. You must have the original receipts and items with you upon departure. Allow a minimum of 30minutes to queue for tax refund at your departure airport. It will be much faster to download the app “TRS AUSTRALIA” and input your purchases there so you just show the QRS code at the tax refund booth.
